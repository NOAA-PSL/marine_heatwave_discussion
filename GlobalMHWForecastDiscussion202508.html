Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - August 2025

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:        

In July 2025, 25% [15%] of the global ocean experienced marine heatwaves (MHWs), which ranked 30th [62nd] among all months since 1991.

Strong MHWs are present over much of the central and western North Pacific, the North Atlantic off the U.S. and European coasts, and Mediterranean Sea. Scattered areas of MHW conditions are also present over much of the Southern Hemisphere.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that global MHW coverage will remain steady at approximately 27% [13%] over the coming year. Note that for many regions, there are currently large decreases in forecasted MHWs likelihoods when removing trends, suggesting that much of the apparent anomalous warmth comes from long-term warming rather than shorter term variability. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical/North Pacific - MHW likelihoods are low in the eastern tropical Pacific and along the North and South American west coasts through boreal fall (0-20% [0-20%]; medium-high confidence). In contrast, MHW likelihoods remain high in the central/western North Pacific (60-80% [30-60%]; low-medium confidence) and in the central/western South Pacific (40-60% [0-30%]; low-medium confidence).
  • Tropical/North Atlantic - Elevated chances of MHWs are forecasted through boreal fall over much of the North Atlantic, extending from central America in the west to the North African and European coasts in the east (50-80% [0-60%]; low-medium confidence).
  • Mediterranean Sea - Forecasts indicate an increased likelihood of MHWs persisting in much of the Mediterranean Sea (30-60% [0-40%]; low confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - Chances of MHW conditions are elevated in the eastern Indian Ocean and Indo-Pacific regions through at least November (50-80% [0-40%]; medium-high confidence).
  • Southern Ocean - High chances of MHWs through November are most pronounced southeast of Africa (80-100% [80-100%] low-medium confidence), There are other scattered regions throughout much of the Southern Ocean that also show elevated MHW probabilities, including south/southeast of Tasmania (80-100% [50-70%]; low confidence) and east of Argentina (60-80% [70-90%]; low confidence).
