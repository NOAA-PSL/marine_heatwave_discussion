Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - June 2025

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:        

In May 2025, 23% [11%] of the global ocean experienced marine heatwaves (MHWs), which ranked 31th [124th] among all months since 1991.

MHW patterns are still characteristic of La Niña, with warm water in the far western tropical Pacific extending both northeastward and southeastward across the subtropical Pacific. MHWs are also found across much of the North Pacific east of Japan, in the Sea of Japan, in the central South Pacific, parts of the North Atlantic west of Europe and in the North Sea, the Caribbean Sea, Mediterranean Sea, parts of  Norwegian Seas, around Australia and in the Indian sector of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage and overall intensity will increase slightly in the next couple of months, with approximately 26-30% [12-14%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions over the 12-month forecast period. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical/North Pacific - MHW likelihoods are low in the eastern tropical Pacific and along the North and South American west coasts through at least August 2025 (0-20% [0-10%]; medium-high confidence). However, MHW likelihoods are high in the central/western North Pacific (60-80% [40-70%]; low-medium confidence) and in the central/western South Pacific (60-90% [0-30%]; low-medium confidence).
  • Tropical/North Atlantic - Elevated chances of MHWs are forecasted into September 2025 extending from the Gulf of Mexico/Caribbean Sea to the eastern North Atlantic as far north as Iceland (30-60% [0-40%]); low-medium confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - Chances of MHW conditions are low over much of the north western Indian Ocean. However, MHWs are more likely in the eastern Indian Ocean through at least September 2025 (50-70% [20-40%]; high confidence). In contrast, existing MHW conditions east of Madagascar are projected to relax through September (medium-high confidence)
  • Southern Ocean - Elevated chances of MHWs are predicted through September of 2025 for the Southern Ocean, primarily in a region southeast of Africa (80-100% [70-100%] low confidence) and south of Australia (70-100% [30-50%] low-medium confidence).
