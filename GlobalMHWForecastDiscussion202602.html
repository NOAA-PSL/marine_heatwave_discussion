Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - February 2026

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:        

In January 2026, 23% [12%] of the global ocean experienced marine heatwaves (MHWs), which ranked 32nd [79th] among all months since 1991.

MHW coverage globally has increased slightly over the past month. Currently, MHWs are scattered across the western tropical Pacific, much of the extratropical Pacific, the western tropical Atlantic, North Atlantic and Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Global MHW coverage is predicted to continue rising over the coming year, to ~30% [15%] by mid-2026 and ~37% [17%] by the end of 2026. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical Pacific - Through May 2026, MHW likelihoods are low in the eastern tropical Pacific (0-20% [0-20%]; high confidence), and elevated in the western tropical Pacific only if warming trends are not removed (50-80% [0-20%]; low-medium confidence). Thereafter, increasing MHW chances in the central and eastern equatorial Pacific are consistent with development of El Niño.
  • North Pacific - Continued high probabilities of MHWs are forecast across the central and western North Pacific (70-100% [30-60%]; low-medium confidence), as well as around Baja California (40-70% [20-50%]; low-medium confidence) for at least the next three months.
  • North Atlantic - Elevated chances of MHWs are forecasted through May over much of the North Atlantic, particularly inside and east of the Gulf of Mexico (40-80% [20-50%]; medium confidence) as well as in the seas surrounding Greenland (50-100% [40-80%]; low confidence).
  • Mediterranean Sea - MHW likelihoods in the Mediterranean through May are projected to remain elevated through May 2026 (30-60% [10-30%]; low-medium confidence).
  • Southern Ocean - Areas of high MHW probabilities over the next several months are scattered across all sectors of the Southern Ocean, with the highest likelihoods southeast of Africa and South America (50-100% [50-80%]; low-medium confidence).
