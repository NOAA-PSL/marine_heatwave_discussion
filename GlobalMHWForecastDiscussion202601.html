Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - January 2026

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:        

In December 2025, 20% [11%] of the global ocean experienced marine heatwaves (MHWs), which ranked 50th [137th] among all months since 1991.

MHW coverage has declined considerably over the past several months. Currently, MHWs are scattered primarily across portions of the northeast, southeast and western tropical Pacific, as well as the North Atlantic and Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Global MHW coverage is predicted to rise over the coming year, to 29% [15%] by mid-2026. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical Pacific - MHW likelihoods through April 2026 are low in the central tropical Pacific (0-10% [0-10%]; high confidence), consistent with La Niña-like conditions. The models predict the development of El Niño conditions into boreal summer, increasing MHW chances in the central and eastern equatorial Pacific into mid-year (40-70% [20-50%]; medium confidence). Western tropical Pacific likelihoods are high due to warming trends, but low if those trends are removed (50-80% [10-30%]; medium confidence).
  • North Pacific - Continued high probabilities of MHWs are forecast across the central and western North Pacific for at least the next three months (70-90% [50-80%]; medium confidence). MHW likelihoods have also increased west of California in the coming months (30-60% [10-30%]; medium confidence).
  • North Atlantic - Elevated chances of MHWs are forecasted into boreal spring over much of the North Atlantic, in a horseshoe shaped swath extending from the Gulf of Mexico in the southwest to the Norwegian Sea in the northeast (60-100% [30-60%]; medium confidence near the Gulf of Mexico and low confidence near Europe).
  • Mediterranean Sea - MHWs in the Mediterranean are projected to continue through at least March 2026 (50-100% [10-50%]; low-medium confidence), though probabilities decline over the length of the forecast.
  • Southern Ocean - Portions of the Southern Ocean are likely to experience MHWs through March 2026, notably at 50-60°S between Africa and Australia (50-100% [40-80%]; low-medium confidence).
